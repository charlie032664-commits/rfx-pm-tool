# v1.4 Demo Script

**Audience:** internal users, leadership, and AI innovation competition judges
**Goal:** show how the tool turns a raw RFQ into a customer-ready compliance
matrix with the PM kept firmly in control.

> Prep before any demo: pick a **case that already has analysis output**
> (`requirements_clean.json` or `requirements_reviewed.json`) and saved
> responses. Do this so the demo never waits on a live extraction. Have the app
> open on **Step 1** with the case selected.

---

## 3-minute demo story

**Theme:** "From RFQ to compliance matrix, with the PM in control."

1. **(0:00–0:20) Frame the problem.**
   "An RFQ arrives as messy Word/Excel/PDF files. A PM normally spends hours
   pulling out requirements and building a compliance matrix by hand."

2. **(0:20–1:00) Show Steps 1–3.**
   Point at the numbered flow. "Step 1 we drop in the source files, Step 2
   pre-checks them, Step 3 the AI engine extracts and cleans the requirements."
   (Use a pre-analyzed case — don't run live.)

3. **(1:00–1:50) Show Step 4 review.**
   Open **Step 4 — Review Requirements.** "This is the human-in-the-loop step.
   The PM edits wording, fixes categories, and can delete junk rows. These edits
   are saved as the *reviewed* requirements."

4. **(1:50–2:30) Show Step 5 + Step 6.**
   "Step 5 is where compliance responses go in. Then **Step 6** — one click —
   builds the matrix. Notice it says *Using reviewed requirements*: the PM's
   edits flow straight through. No AI re-run, no waiting."

5. **(2:30–3:00) Show Step 7 + close.**
   Download `compliance_matrix.xlsx`, open it. "Customer-ready in seconds. The AI
   did the heavy lifting; the PM stayed in control of every word that ships."

---

## 5-minute demo story

Everything in the 3-minute version, plus deeper beats:

1. **(0:00–0:30) Problem + cost.**
   Quantify it: "A typical RFQ has 100–200 requirements. Manual matrix building
   is a half-day to a full day of error-prone copy-paste."

2. **(0:30–1:30) Steps 1–3 with detail.**
   Show Step 2 pre-check catching a problem before any analysis is spent.
   Explain Step 3 as the **AI extraction + normalization engine**.

3. **(1:30–2:45) Step 4 review — the differentiator.**
   Edit a requirement's wording live. Delete an obviously-junk row. Recategorize
   one. Emphasize: "This is *governed* AI — nothing reaches the customer without
   PM sign-off."

4. **(2:45–3:45) Step 5 responses.**
   Fill a compliance status + vendor comment on one or two rows. Explain that
   responses are saved per case and reused on every regenerate.

5. **(3:45–4:30) Step 6 source priority + instant regenerate.**
   Click Generate. Call out the *"Using reviewed requirements."* banner. Then say:
   "If I tweak a response and regenerate, it's instant — because Step 6 is a pure
   export. No LLM, no pipeline re-run."

6. **(4:30–5:00) Step 7 + vision.**
   Download and open the matrix. Close with the agentic framing (see pitch doc):
   "Each step is really an agent — and this is the MVP of an agent platform."

## What to say for each step

- **Step 1 — Case & Source Files:** "Everything is organized per RFQ case."
- **Step 2 — Pre-check:** "Fail fast — catch missing/invalid files up front."
- **Step 3 — Analyze RFQ:** "This is the AI extraction engine. It reads messy
  documents and produces structured, cleaned requirements."
- **Advanced option: Improve requirement wording with AI** (right after Step 3,
  before Step 4): "An optional, collapsed AI step that rewrites fragment-style
  requirements into complete, standalone form — without changing the `req_id` or
  the original text. We placed it here so wording cleanup happens between
  extraction and PM review. For the demo we leave it collapsed."
- **Step 4 — Review Requirements:** "The control point. AI proposes; the PM
  disposes. Edits and deletions are saved as the reviewed source of truth."
- **Step 5 — Fill Compliance Responses:** "Owners answer each requirement.
  Responses persist and are merged on every export."
- **Step 6 — Generate Compliance Matrix:** "A deterministic export. It picks the
  most-reviewed requirement source and merges responses — no LLM, no pipeline."
- **Step 7 — Download Results:** "The primary deliverable, ready to send."

---

## What NOT to click during the demo

- ❌ **Do not run Step 3 "Analyze RFQ" live** — extraction can take time and may
  call the LLM. Always demo on a pre-analyzed case.
- ❌ **Do not trigger the Full Pipeline** / any "run everything" action.
- ❌ **Do not open Advanced / Diagnostics** expanders (cache, logs, raw JSON) —
  they distract from the PM story.
- ❌ **Do not run the *Advanced option: Improve requirement wording with AI***
  (the requirement Normalization / LLM action, now in a collapsed expander right
  after Step 3). It's safe to expand and show, but don't trigger it live.
- ❌ **Do not edit `.env` or model settings** in front of the audience.
- ✅ Safe to click freely: Step 4 edits, Step 5 responses, **Step 6 Generate**,
  Step 7 download — these are fast and don't call the LLM.

> If asked "can it run live?": yes — but say you're using a prepared case so the
> demo stays tight. Offer a live extraction as a follow-up, not on the clock.

---

## How to explain "AI Engine / Agent-ready workflow"

Say it like this:

> "Each numbered step is really a specialized **agent**. A Source File agent
> ingests the documents, an Extraction agent pulls out requirements, a
> Normalization agent cleans the language, a Review agent supports the PM, a
> Response agent handles compliance answers, and a Matrix Generation agent
> assembles the deliverable. Today they run as a guided workflow with a human in
> the loop; the architecture is ready to let these agents run more autonomously
> as we trust each one."

Keep it concrete: point at the actual steps on screen as you name the agents.

---

## How to explain human-in-the-loop review

> "The AI never ships anything to the customer on its own. Step 4 is a hard
> checkpoint: the PM reviews, edits, and can delete any requirement before it
> reaches the matrix. Deleted rows never appear; PM wording always wins. That's
> what makes this safe to use on real customer-facing RFQs — it's **governed
> automation**, not a black box."

One-liner for judges: *"AI proposes, the PM disposes, and the matrix only ever
contains what the PM approved."*
